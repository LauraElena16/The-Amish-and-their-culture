The Amish

                 The Amish are people who are part of a Christian religious community with Anabaptist doctrine. Although most members of the Amish community have Dutch roots, their church appeared in Switzerland in 1693. In the 18th century, they fled to the United States where they still live, but a small part is found in the Canadian province of Ontario.

                 The Amish community is known especially for the life they live, this being a simple, traditional but also isolated from the modern world, for the simple clothing worn in the seventeenth century, but also for the power of opposition to any form of modernity such as would be the adoption of technology in everyday life in order not to waste time spent with family and encourage face-to-face conversations. 

                  Their lives have strict rules. Whoever deviates from them can receive the sect's harshest punishment: exile in the world of sinners in modern society. Some communities will permanently isolate those who have chosen to leave, even if they have moved to other Amish congregations. Others no longer support this tradition by choosing to keep in touch with those who have left the community.    

                   This community believes in the New Testament, believing that the Bible is the only religious authority it must follow. "Ordnung" is a set of religious and civil rules. Amish communities do not have a central government, with each group having the right to create its own version of the rules. These are derived from the Bible, and those that are not are justified by urging a person not to go out of God's word. 

                     As a maternal language, the Amish speak Dutch or Pennsylvania German, but also tend to borrow English words. Of the early communities that settled in the United States, many gave up their Amish identity and became Mennonites. As an occupation, they are excellent farmers and farmers and most of their communities support themselves. They are also famous craftsmen; their products being highly sought after for their quality.
