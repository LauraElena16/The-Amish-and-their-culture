The Amish and their culture

Generalities

Amish  are people who are part of a Christian religious community with Anabaptist doctrine.    

The Amish community is known especially for the life they live, this being a simple, traditional, but also isolated from the modern world, for the simple clothing worn in the seventeenth century, as well as the power of opposition to any form of modernity.

History

The history of the Amish com​munity begins in 1693, in Switzerland, with the division made by Jacob Amman into the Mennonite community. The faction he led took its name (Amman - Amish). Jacob Amman instituted a strict code of rules to guide the community, "Ordnung."

After a crucial moment in which the Amish decided to face the leadership of the community, these people were divided into two major orders. The "old" one that has remained true to all initial perceptions and a "new" one that largely accepts social change and innovation in technology and science, but retains some of the traditions.

Today, there are approximately 280,000 old believers living in more than 200 small settlements located both in the United States and Canada.

Religious practice

Two important religious rituals in Amish life are baptism and communion.

Baptism

As Anabaptists, the Amish attach great importance to adult baptism, so that most young people who take the oath of baptism are between 18 and 22 years old. This lifelong promise to God and the members of their community means that they will be accountable to the church for the rest of their lives.

Baptism

Communion

Communion is allowed only to those who have been baptized. The sacrament service lasts eight hours and includes preaching, a light meal during the service, and the commemoration of Christ's death with bread and wine. The ritual ends with the members washing and drying each other on their feet.

The way of life

Family an​d personal life

The Amish family is the basis of the Amish way of life, which is guided by the unwritten Ordnung plan, which guides them through all the details of daily life.

Amish men and women usually take on traditional gender roles. Husbands are primarily responsible for the financial well-being of the family. Wives usually dedicate themselves to housekeeping and motherhood.

School and education

Part of Amish education consists of eight years in a one-room school taught by an eighth-grade teacher.

English, reading, writing, arithmetic and history are taught in the Amish school. These are considered all the skills needed to live an Amish life and yet be able to do business with the outside world.

After the eighth grade, Amish education moves from school learning to home and community practices.

Wedding tradition

Their weddings followed a rather strict tradition. The ceremony was allowed to be done only on Tuesdays and Thursdays, but this custom was lost with the passage of time. The event is attended by a large number of people (200-600) and the food served is traditional. After the wedding, the couple is supported by their parents until they move to their home.

Amish clothing

The main reason why Amish are called ordinary people is the way they dress, their clothes being simple, without patterns, and the rules require the use of only solid colors such as black, blue, burgundy, brown, purple or green.

Men

The men's clothing consisted of a white shirt, a black suit and a hay or felt hat. Also, married men can easily be recognized by the beard they wear, whereas unmarried men are not allowed to grow a beard.

Women

When it comes to Amish women's wardrobes, their wardrobes are quite small, consisting of only four dresses. The dresses are solid in color with long sleeves. Around the farm and the house, women walk barefoot and wear a gray apron. Also, women never cut their hair and can hide it under a cover.

Furniture

Amish furniture, made entirely of wood, is manually sculpted by Amish people. The styles most often used by Amish carpenters are generally traditional, and special attention is paid to details.

Music

The songs are mainly with patriotic or religious themes and they consist in a collection of hymns written by the first Anabaptists, this written music not being used during worship. In addition to patriotic and religious anthems, Amish people also sing popular hymns such as youth songs and family gathering songs.

Life in the modern world

According to their beliefs, Amish people should not conform to the world. For this reason, they avoid certain modern facilities that would not allow them to adhere to their beliefs. Amish do not use electricity so there are no TVs, computers or radios

In addition, the use of the telephone is restricted, claiming that privacy and face-to-face communication are lost. Because of this, a single telephone is used for several houses located away from them in a cabin.

Members do not own cars but are allowed to travel by bus and train to reach more distant destinations. They are also forbidden to travel by plane, being considered a too modern mean of transport.
